Dereck Villagrana — Product Designer

Good design doesn't announce itself. It just works — and then you notice its absence the moment it's gone. That's the kind of work Dereck has been chasing since he first opened Figma: interfaces that feel obvious in hindsight, systems that earn trust without asking for it, experiences that don't need an instruction manual.

He's spent the last few years moving through different design contexts — building within a student design club, shipping under hackathon pressure, and taking on freelance work where the brief arrived late and the deadline didn't. Along the way he picked up enough code to build his own tools and, eventually, this portfolio — hand-written HTML, CSS, and JS, no Webflow, no Framer. It turns out the gap between design and development is narrower when you're willing to learn both sides.

Philosophy

"Design is a series of decisions most people will never notice — and that's the goal."

  • Clarity over cleverness. If you have to explain it, it's not done.
  • Constraints are assets. A tight brief forces a sharper idea.
  • The work isn't finished when it's built — it's finished when someone gets it the first time.
