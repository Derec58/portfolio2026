# BeReal Redesign
## BeRealer Than Ever Before

Revamping BeReal's social experience to boost long-term engagement, without compromising its core commitment to authenticity.

---

**Timeline:** 6 Weeks, 2023
**Team:** Dereck Villagrana, Pooja Deshmukh, Bea Zhu, Andy Do, Reece Kuramoto
**Tools:** Figma
**My Role:** UX Designer, Prototyping Lead
**Award:** Best Prototype, UC Davis Design Interactive

---

### Overview

In 2022, BeReal went from a niche app to a global hit, reaching around 73.5 million monthly active users at its peak with roughly 20 million people opening it every day. But by late 2022 and into 2023, growth stalled. Downloads declined quarter-over-quarter, early adopters dropped off, and the app that once felt refreshingly different started to feel repetitive.

For UC Davis's Design Interactive, our team of five took on a six-week sprint to understand why people were drifting away and how BeReal could win them back without becoming the very thing it stood against.

---

### The Problem

BeReal's viral moment didn't translate into a lasting habit.

By August 2022, BeReal had 73.5 million monthly active users and an estimated 20 million daily actives. But by Q4 2022, downloads fell for the first time since 2021. U.S. monthly active users dropped from a 3.7M peak in November 2022 to just over 3M by August 2023. Meanwhile, Snapchat, Instagram, and TikTok had already copied BeReal's dual-camera mechanic and folded it into much richer ecosystems, making it easier than ever for users to drift.

Three core friction points:

**Repetition:** The single daily post format made the app feel like "same photo, different day." Without lightweight ways to play, react, or remix, there wasn't much to do once you'd posted.

**Social pressure:** Posting unfiltered content to your entire friends list every day created real anxiety. BeReal's authenticity premise and the pressure to present well were in direct conflict, with no release valve.

**A forgotten Discovery page:** Discovery existed, but users described it as random and irrelevant. With no way to control what they saw, it wasn't worth opening.

**Problem Statement:** BeReal needs to increase long-term engagement by giving users more meaningful, low-pressure ways to participate and connect, while staying true to its authenticity-first mission and avoiding addictive, ad-driven patterns.

### Research

**Literature and Market Review**

- BeReal's MAU grew over 2,000% from January to September 2022, and it won iPhone App of the Year
- By Q4 2022, quarterly downloads had dropped to ~31.5 million, and retention was increasingly under scrutiny
- BeReal explicitly rejects ads, influencer culture, and addictive design, a constraint we had to design within, not around

Quote: "The app's success ultimately depends on its ability to balance authenticity with safety and scale up its user base." — Brooke Erin Duffy, Cornell Professor

**Competitive Analysis**

| Platform | Core Strengths | What BeReal Lacked by Comparison |
|---|---|---|
| Snapchat | Ephemeral messaging, AR Lenses, Snap Map, streaks | High-frequency, low-stakes interactions that build habits |
| Instagram | Multi-format ecosystem: Feed, Stories, Reels, Close Friends, DMs | Multiple ways to express yourself, archive content, and reach different audiences |
| TikTok | Personalized For You feed, Duets, remixing, trends | Strong passive and active engagement loops that keep people returning |

**User Surveys and Interviews**

- 35 survey participants via Qualtrics
- 6 in-depth generative interviews with BeReal users aged 17-24
- **76%** never use Discovery
- **67%** always retake their BeReals before posting
- **Only 9%** try to post on time

User quotes:
> "It can get repetitive and boring because it's only one post per day."
> "I like the intention behind the app being less addictive compared to Instagram."
> "Even though the purpose is to be authentic, I still felt the need to curate my post of the day."

### Key Insights

Three pain points from affinity mapping:
1. Posts are becoming repetitive and boring
2. Users aren't posting on time. Social pressure is the real blocker.
3. The Discovery page is rarely touched and doesn't feel worth opening

### Features

**Onboarding:** Welcome tour for returning users walking through each new feature on first login after the update.

**BeReal Prompts:** Optional daily challenges (e.g., "Make a silly face") that attach to your BeReal. Users can also submit prompts to be randomly assigned to friends.

**BeCloser:** A close friends circle that lets users share certain posts with a smaller, private group. Circles can be created and edited anytime without notifying anyone.

**Memory Highlights:** Pin up to 7 past BeReals on your profile. Revamped memories calendar with Week, Month, Year views.

**Discovery 2.0:** Curate your own Discovery by choosing up to 3 countries per day. Globe-spin mechanic for serendipitous discovery.

---

### Usability Testing Results

**BeReal Prompts** (6 users)
- Users liked that prompts made posting feel less mundane
- When a prompt was declined it disappeared. Users wanted a way to revisit it.
- Home screen felt cluttered; share navigation wasn't obvious
- Fix: Added a persistent prompt entry point and simplified the layout

**BeCloser** (8 users)
- Layout made it unclear where close-friends content lived
- Lightning-bolt icon didn't communicate "inner circle"
- 50/50 split on whether they'd use it
- Fix: Redesigned the icon, added in-context onboarding, simplified navigation

**Memory Highlights** (6 users)
- Week/Month/Year calendar felt intuitive right away
- Users liked pinning favorite moments on their profile
- Highlight upload flow felt hidden
- Fix: Embedded the flow more naturally with clearer step-by-step guidance

**Discovery 2.0** (10 users)
- Users liked exploring different parts of the world
- Too many search and filter options up front felt overwhelming
- Users weren't sure what choosing countries meant without more context
- Fix: Leaned into the globe as the primary interaction, reduced visible controls, added a clear "3 countries per day" indicator

### Reflection

Designing within BeReal's authenticity ethos was the defining constraint of this project, and the most valuable learning experience. Having a clear product philosophy forced us to say no to easy engagement wins and treat constraints as a design tool rather than a limitation.

**Designing for behavior, not just screens.** Early on it was tempting to "fix engagement" by adding more notifications or feeds. But when 67% of users are already retaking photos before posting, and only 9% post on time, the problem isn't the UI. It's the underlying pressure. That pushed us toward lowering the stakes through prompts, smaller circles, and curated discovery rather than just adding more things to tap.

**Respecting what makes a product distinct.** BeReal was surrounded by giants with far more features. Competing on volume would've undermined the whole point. The better question was how to make BeReal's niche feel richer without making it feel like everything else.

**Iteration is where real learning happens.** Moving fast from research to testable prototypes taught me how often your assumptions are wrong. BeCloser split users right down the middle at 50/50. That kind of feedback only comes from getting something in front of real people early.

If I could go back, I'd spend more time on Discovery 2.0. Even after two rounds of testing it felt more like a clever idea than a fully validated direction. I'd want to explore whether relationship-based filters, not just geography, could create a stronger pull and actually measure whether it moved the needle on return visits.
